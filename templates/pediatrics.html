Assessment

Assessment Sequence

Remember to use age-appropriate techniques and involve the family:

  1. Observe before touching
  2. Use distraction techniques
  3. Start with least invasive assessments
  4. Save painful procedures for last

Common Conditions

High-Priority Conditions

  • Respiratory: Asthma, Bronchiolitis, Croup
  • Infectious: Otitis Media, Chickenpox, Meningitis
  • Gastrointestinal: Gastroenteritis, Appendicitis
  • Developmental: ADHD, Autism Spectrum Disorders

Question: A 2-year-old presents with barking cough and inspiratory stridor. Which condition should the nurse suspect?

  1. Pneumonia
  2. Asthma
  3. Croup
  4. Bronchiolitis
Answer: C - Croup

Rationale: Barking cough and inspiratory stridor are classic symptoms of croup (laryngotracheobronchitis).

Medications

Medication Safety

  • Always calculate doses based on weight (kg)
  • Double-check all calculations
  • Use appropriate delivery methods for age
  • Monitor closely for side effects

Common Calculations

Weight-based dosing: mg/kg/dose or mg/kg/day

  • Convert weight to kg (divide pounds by 2.2)
  • Calculate single dose
  • Check maximum dose limits

Family-Centered Care

Key Principles

Family-centered care recognizes the family as constant in the child's life and essential to care planning.

Implementation Strategies

  • Include family in care planning
  • Provide education and support
  • Respect cultural values
  • Promote parent-child bonding
  • Support siblings and extended family

Question: Which nursing action best demonstrates family-centered care?

  1. Limiting visiting hours to protect the child
  2. Making decisions without consulting parents
  3. Including parents in daily rounds and care planning
  4. Restricting sibling visits
Answer: C - Including parents in daily rounds and care planning

Rationale: Including parents in rounds and care planning recognizes them as essential partners in their child's care.
