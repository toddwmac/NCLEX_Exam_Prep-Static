Fundamentals of Nursing

About This Guide

This comprehensive guide covers essential nursing fundamentals you need to know for the NCLEX exam. Each section includes key concepts, practice questions, and clinical pearls.

Nursing Process

ADPIE Mnemonic

  • Assessment - Collect and analyze patient data
  • Diagnosis - Identify actual/potential health problems
  • Planning - Set goals and outcomes
  • Implementation - Perform nursing interventions
  • Evaluation - Measure effectiveness of care

Question: During which phase of the nursing process would the nurse establish measurable patient outcomes?

  1. Assessment
  2. Diagnosis
  3. Planning
  4. Implementation
Answer: C - Planning

Rationale: The planning phase involves setting specific, measurable goals and expected outcomes for the patient.

  • Respirations: 12-20 breaths/minute
  • Blood Pressure: 120/80 mmHg (normal adult)
  • O2 Saturation: 95-100%

Assessment Tips

  • Count pulse and respirations for full 60 seconds if irregular
  • Use appropriate BP cuff size
  • Document position and site of measurements
  • Note factors affecting readings (exercise, anxiety, medications)

Safety and Infection Control

Standard Precautions

  • Hand hygiene
  • Personal protective equipment (PPE)
  • Safe injection practices
  • Respiratory hygiene/cough etiquette
  • Clean and disinfected environmental surfaces

Hand Hygiene Moments

Remember the WHO's 5 moments for hand hygiene:

  1. Before patient contact
  2. Before aseptic procedure
  3. After body fluid exposure
  4. After patient contact
  5. After contact with patient surroundings

Basic Care and Comfort

Activities of Daily Living (ADLs)

  • Bathing and hygiene
  • Dressing and grooming
  • Eating and drinking
  • Toileting
  • Mobility and transfers

Question: A nurse is caring for a patient who requires assistance with bathing. Which action should the nurse implement first?

  1. Begin washing the patient's face
  2. Assess the patient's ability to participate
  3. Fill the basin with water
  4. Remove the patient's gown
Answer: B - Assess the patient's ability to participate

Rationale: Assessment should always be the first step to determine the level of assistance needed and promote patient independence.

Documentation

Documentation Principles

  • Be objective, accurate, and timely
  • Use approved abbreviations
  • Document in chronological order
  • Never alter or destroy records
  • Follow facility policies

SBAR Communication

  • Situation - What is happening now?
  • Background - What led up to this?
  • Assessment - What do you think is going on?
  • Recommendation - What should be done?

Medication Administration

Rights of Medication Administration

  • Right patient
  • Right medication
  • Right dose
  • Right route
  • Right time
  • Right documentation
  • Right to refuse
  • Right assessment
  • Right education
  • Right evaluation

Question: A nurse is preparing to administer medications. Which of the "rights" of medication administration is being followed when the nurse checks the patient's allergy bracelet?

  1. Right patient
  2. Right assessment
  3. Right medication
  4. Right documentation
Answer: B - Right assessment

Rationale: Checking the allergy bracelet is part of the assessment process to ensure safe medication administration.
